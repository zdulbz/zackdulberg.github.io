About

A Unique Path Through
Science & Medicine

Trained as both a physician and a computational scientist, I work at the intersection of artificial intelligence and human cognition—using machine learning to illuminate the mechanisms of the mind.

My academic journey began with undergraduate studies in Physics and Physiology at McGill University, followed by medical training at the University of Toronto and Family Medicine residency at the University of Ottawa.

Driven by questions about how the brain processes information, I then pursued a PhD at the Princeton Neuroscience Institute under the mentorship of Dr. Jonathan Cohen, focusing on reinforcement learning, neural networks, and cognitive science.

Currently, I maintain a clinical practice in mental health as well as concussion rehab, and I serve as a Senior Fellow at the Network Contagion Research Institute, where I apply computational methods to understand information threats, social media dynamics, and the psychological factors underlying online behavior.
